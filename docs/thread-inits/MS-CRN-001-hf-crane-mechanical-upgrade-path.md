# THREAD INIT: HF Crane Mechanical Upgrade Path

## Purpose

This thread is for staged mechanical upgrades to the Harbor Freight / Pittsburgh 2-ton foldable shop crane, item 58755.

The original lathe lift mission is complete. The crane is now a material-handling platform and possible future Crane of Theseus refactor.

## Project Context

The crane was bought to lift and place a South Bend lathe. That mission is complete.

The next direction is not panic repair. It is disciplined staged improvement:

- better fasteners
- better pins
- better caster assemblies
- custom legs
- outriggers and stabilizers
- possible boom extension improvements
- possible future powered hydraulic module

## Working Doctrine

Separate mobility from stability.

Casters are for moving the crane.

Outriggers, chocks, jacks, feet, cribbing, and load discipline are for lifting mode.

Do not pretend caster brakes are a structural safety system.

## Known Crane Facts

Use project files if available:

- Harbor Freight manual PDF for item 58755
- HF 2-ton crane measurement register CSV or XLSX
- Metric shoulder bolt size chart PDF

Manual reference values include:

- rated capacity: 2 tons, 4000 lb
- crane weight: about 197.1 lb
- maximum height: 90.5 in
- minimum height: 17.75 in
- boom length range: about 41 in to 62.4 in
- stock caster mix includes 3 in swivel casters and 3.5 in swivel casters, including braked rear casters
- many factory fasteners are M8, M14, and M16
- manual dimensions are reference only; actual crane measurement wins

## Upgrade Philosophy

Prefer:

- smooth clevis pins or custom smooth pins for primary joints
- no threads in shear
- reamed holes where pin fit matters
- A500 structural tube for legs, boom-related tube work, outriggers, and receivers
- A36 plate or flat bar for tabs, gussets, adapter plates, doubler plates, and caster plates
- common caster plate patterns for future serviceability
- polyurethane-on-iron or polyurethane-on-steel caster wheels for serious mobility
- screw-down or cam-deploy stabilizer feet for lifting mode
- chocks and cribbing as part of the crane kit, not random scraps

Avoid:

- relying on split lock washers for critical rotating or pin-retention functions
- mystery soft rubber wheels for static lathe loads
- pure pneumatic lifting
- lowering systems without controlled descent
- custom one-off dimensions where common pin and caster families would work
- letting every joint use a different almost-compatible pin size

## Candidate BOM Buckets

Organize the upgrade path around:

- structural tube
- A36 plate, tabs, gussets, and adapter plates
- clevis pins, custom smooth pins, retainers, washers, and bushings
- complete caster assemblies
- caster mounting hardware
- outriggers
- stabilizer feet or jacks
- chocks and floor pads
- rigging interface parts
- slings, shackles, hook, spreader, and edge protection
- drill bits, reamers, transfer punches, and layout tooling
- witness-marking and inspection supplies

## Pin Size Doctrine

Use standard families, not random near-miss sizes.

Possible classes:

- main structural crane pins: 5/8 in or 16 mm, choose one
- medium accessory and outrigger pins: 1/2 in or 12 mm, choose one
- small service hardware: M8 or 5/16 in, depending subsystem

Design so lower-class pins cannot be accidentally installed in higher-load structural locations.

## First Task

Build a staged roadmap:

1. immediate KISS improvements
2. measurement-dependent purchases
3. bolt-on upgrades
4. fabrication-dependent upgrades
5. future v2 crane or cart-module upgrades

Then turn that into BOM buckets with buy-now, measure-first, and defer labels.
